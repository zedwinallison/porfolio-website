Blender Day 1 Tutorial

I created this video as an introductory lesson to introduce Blender to someone who has never touched a 3D software before. Many skills were deployed in the creation of this video, including writing, editing, animation, illustration, voice-over, and expertise in structuring an entertaining video. This was not a collaborative project whatsoever; every aspect was recorded and edited by me.

A great deal of planning went into the structure of this video. It's important to consider the viewer's perspective, and particularly their attention when structuring and editing a video. The first few seconds are the most important, and as they watch, their likelihood to click away steadily decreases. For an educational video, prioritizing entertainment, comedy, and appealing visuals means that the viewer will retain more information. You will notice the sound design in this video as well. Great care went into using sound to capture attention, and seamlessly transition between sections in interesting ways. This is the video that I wish I had when I was initially learning how to use Blender. I wanted to make a seemingly complex software seem very approachable.

How to Make Title Cards

This video was created as a study of title cards in film and TV. It breaks down visual design choices, and film editing concepts/practices. This video was recorded, illustrated, animated, and edited by me. There are no other collaborators. My goal was to capture a viewer’s attention and educate while providing entertainment that is engaging, visually interesting, cinematic, and comedic. It's meant to be quite silly and not be taken too seriously, but still leave the viewer with a valuable takeaway.
